Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Open your app in a new tab: http://localhost:8081
Step 2: Open DevTools (F12 or Cmd+Option+I)
Step 3: Go to the Network tab
Step 4: Clear the network log (trash icon or Cmd+Shift+R)
Step 5: Reload the page (F5 or Cmd+R)
Step 6: In the Network tab, look for:
  • Type: "document" or "html"
  • Name: Should be just "/" or "localhost:8081"
  • Status: Should be 200
Step 7: Click on that request, then:
  • Go to the Response tab
  • Copy the HTML content
  • Or take a screenshot of the first 50-100 lines

What to Look For:

The HTML should contain things like:

  • <html> tag
  • <head> section
  • <script> tags loading your app
  • References to index.bundle or App.web.js

Important:

DO NOT look at requests to:

  • ogs.google.com (Google services)
  • chrome-extension:// (Browser extensions)
  • apis.google.com (Google APIs)

These are NOT your app's HTML!

Alternative: View Page Source

If the Network tab is confusing, try this:

  1. Open your app: http://localhost:8081
  2. Right-click on the page ‚Üí "View Page Source"
  3. Or press Cmd+Option+U (Mac) or Ctrl+U (Windows)
  4. Copy the first 100-200 lines of HTML
