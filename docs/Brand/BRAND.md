# Wake — Brand Identity

Last updated: 2026-04-07.

---

## What Wake Believes

The best thing that can happen in fitness is a real person — someone genuinely excellent — choosing to bring you into the way they train. Not a PDF. Not an algorithm. A person.

We are data-first. We believe in tracking, measuring, and using information to make better decisions about how you move and recover. But data is only as good as the person interpreting it with you.

We build in public. We make mistakes in front of people. We tell stories. We document what's actually happening — not a polished version, the real one. Storytelling is central to who we are.

---

## The Enemy

**Parasocial fitness.**

You follow someone for years. Watch every video. Buy the generic plan. And train completely alone. There is no relationship — only the illusion of one.

Combined with:

**Fitness as performance for the algorithm.** Content optimized for engagement, not results. Plans built for mass distribution, not for you. The noise of a thousand strangers telling you what to do, none of them knowing anything about you.

Wake exists in direct opposition to all of this. Real training happens when you trust the person on the other side. When they know your data, your history, your goals. When they built the program because of who you are — not despite not knowing you.

---

## The Athletes

This is what we call our creators. Not coaches, not influencers — athletes.

They are the engine of everything that happens on Wake. They are the stars of the show.

**Regular people that just happen to be awesome.**

Not untouchable. Not celebrities performing fitness for an audience. The person you wish was your friend who happens to be an incredible coach. They have their own philosophy, their own way of training, their own story. And the value proposition is simple: you get to actually train with them.

They are close to the people who train with them. Accessible. Real. The relationship doesn't feel transactional — it feels like being let into something.

Think of how BPN (Bear Performance Nutrition) treats their athletes: real people with real lives who happen to be extraordinary. That is the model. Wake empowers regular people to do extraordinary things, and then lets others be part of that.

---

## The Wake Person

Serious about health but not obsessive about it. They train hard — and they train with their friends. They use data to understand their body. They listen to Huberman Lab. They've read about sleep, recovery, nutrition. They might wear a Whoop.

But they also have a great time. They prioritize relationships. They're building things — careers, products, themselves. They are polymaths: curious, active, interested in many things at once.

When you see a Wake person, you don't think "they're fit." You think "they're interesting."

This is the aspiration for the community Wake builds: not a status symbol, but a social symbol. A signal of identity. The way another person with a Whoop on their wrist tells you something about how they think about their life — Wake tells you something about the kind of person someone is. Fun. Intentional. Connected.

---

## The Community

Not peer-to-peer. Not creator-to-client. Something bigger.

An identity. A signal. When someone is part of Wake, they associate with a certain kind of person — someone that prioritizes their health, cares about the people around them, takes their training seriously without taking themselves too seriously.

The community starts with Colombian users first — but the aspiration is cosmopolitan. Not regional. The Wake person could be in Bogotá, Mexico City, Miami, or Madrid. The culture is pan-Latin in its warmth and social energy, but global in its values and references.

## Brand Voice

- Raw. We show the inside — the messy, unfinished, real version.
- Honest. We make mistakes in public and own them.
- Story-first. Every athlete, every user, every feature has a story worth telling.
- Human. Not corporate. Never polished for the sake of appearing polished.
- Confident, not arrogant. We know what we're building and why.

---

## Visual Identity (Direction)

- Dark cinematic. Canvas: `#1a1a1a`. Content in white opacity tones.
- No hardcoded accent color — extracted dynamically from content.
- No gold. No gradients as decoration.
- All entrances: fade + translateY. Spring easing `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`.
- Feels intentional and deliberate. Every element earns its place.
- References: Cursor, Linear, Loom for structural clarity. The visual weight of high-end athletic editorial.
